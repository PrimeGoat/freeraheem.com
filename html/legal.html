BANK FRAUD CASE:
  • Manhattan prosecutors argued that bank fraud occurred based on an account Brennerman opened for a few weeks with an institution, Morgan Stanley Smith Barney, LLC which is not a bank. Brennerman deposited his own money and did not ask for any loan and did not receive any money. The prosecutors pivoted and the Judge argued that Brennerman received "perks" worth $6,000 such as sky miles and fancy credit card, even though the credit card was closed with zero balance and Brennerman did not have the account open long enough to even earn any sky miles. The law says that prosecutors cannot prosecute bank fraud unless the account is with an institution which is FDIC insured, however the Court and prosecutors ignored the law. In fact, when Brennerman highlighted it to the judge he just ignored Brennerman.
  • Judge Richard J. Sullivan then engaged in a surreptitious endeavor to falsely satisfy the FDIC essential element necessary to convict Brennerman for bank fraud and bank fraud conspiracy by improperly supplanting a non-FDIC insured institution, Morgan Stanley Smith Barney, LLC "MSSB" (which Government presented as Government Exhibit - GX1-57; GX1-57A; GX529; GX1-73, during trial as evidence of Brennerman`s interaction with Morgan Stanley (see 1:17-CR-337-RJS, doc. no. 167)) with a FDIC insured institution, Morgan Stanley Private Bank "MSPB" (even though Government presented no evidence of Brennerman`s interaction with MSPB (see 1:17-CR-337-RJS, doc. no 206), in an endeavor to wrongly convict and imprison Brennerman.
  • On appeal, the Second Circuit Federal Manhattan Court ("Second Circuit") ignored trial testimony from Government witnesses, Barry Gonzalez and Kevin Bonebrake, who testified that Morgan Stanley & Co, LLC is not FDIC-insured and operates through various subsidiaries; that Brennerman opened his wealth management account with Morgan Stanley Smith Barney, LLC, a non-FDIC insured subsidiary of Morgan Stanley & Co, LLC; and that each subsidiary requires its own FDIC certificate as the FDIC certificate of one subsidiary does not cover another subsidiary or the parent company (see 1:17-CR-337-RJS, trial transcripts 384-385; 387-388; 409; 1057-1061). Instead the Second Circuit generalized by stating that Brennerman defrauded "Morgan Stanley, an FDIC insured institution" without considering the trial records. The Second Circuit also stated incorrectly that the bank fraud was about a single phone call which Brennerman had with Kevin Bonebrake about oil asset financing without acknowledging trial testimony where Mr. Bonebrake told the Court that the call was "very preliminary discussion" and that the company where he worked was not expected to provide any money but instead expected to help raise financing from investors. Here as well, the law says that the prosecutors cannot prosecute Brennerman unless his phone call was with someone that worked for a bank. Kevin Bonebrake worked for another non-FDIC insured subsidiary of Morgan Stanley. The Court and prosecutors ignored the law.

SYSTEMIC OPPRESSION AND RACIAL INJUSTICE WITHIN THE CRIMINAL JUSTICE SYSTEM

This was not a prosecution, it was a deliberate endeavor to ensure that I was arrested, prosecuted and imprisoned highlighting the systemic oppression and racial injustice that minorities face.

[ + ]
Federal Judge and Prosecutors ignored the law to prosecute and imprison an innocent black man for a crime of criminal contempt of court.

In 2016, Judge Lewis A. Kaplan ignored the law when he Googled me (which is wrong because Federal rule prohibits Judges from conducting research by themselves since they are only required to make ruling based on the law and evidence presented) then held me in civil contempt of court even though I was not part of the case ("non-party"). I was never in court and I did not personally participate in the case and no court orders were personally issued to me, yet Judge Kaplan felt above the law to hold me in civil contempt of court. Then he referred me to the Manhattan prosecutors in the Southern District of New York and persuaded them to prosecute me criminally. The Manhattan prosecutors equally ignored the law and on Judge Kaplan`s orders arrested me and charged me with criminal contempt of court, even without ever being part of the civil case.

I was granted bail in April 2017, and within three weeks without any investigation the Manhattan prosecutors indicted me for fraud based on the civil case. This time the fraud case was assigned to Judge Richard J. Sullivan who decided to detain and imprison me prior to trial. To prepare for trial, I asked the Manhattan prosecutors for evidence but they told me that they had never requested or obtained the evidence which was surprising because I did not understand how they could claim there was fraud without ever reviewing the evidence. Their excuse was that the evidence which I needed to defend myself was in London, U.K. and that they were not obligated to obtain it. More sobering was the Court`s denial of my request to compel for the evidence. The Court told me that since the evidence was in London, U.K. I could not issue subpoena to compel for the evidence.

I proceeded to trial for criminal contempt of court without evidence I needed to demonstrate to the jury that even though the court orders were not directed to me personally, I was doing my best on behalf of the company to negotiate settlement with the Chinese bank since the court order stated to settle and representatives of the bank advised me that they were more interested in settlement than receiving documents which the orders had asked the company to provide. To make matters worse Judge Kaplan then allowed the Manhattan prosecutors to show the jury that he had already held me in civil contempt without explaining to them that he did it against the law or that I was not even in the country at the time when he held me in civil contempt and that I was not a party to the civil case itself. The jury decided that since the Judge presiding over the case had already held me in civil contempt then I must be guilty of criminal contempt. This was very wrong because the United States Constitution guarantees everyone the equal protection of the law but Judge Kaplan ignored the law to commence my prosecution. The Constitution also guarantees that every defendant is able to compel for evidence and witnesses in their favor during trial however both the Judge and the Manhattan prosecutors ensured that I did not gain access to the evidence which I required to defend myself and present a complete defense.

If Judge Kaplan had followed the Federal Rule by not Googling me and the law by not holding me in contempt as a non-party then I would never have been arrested, prosecuted and imprisoned for an offence I did not commit.


[ + ]
Federal Judge and Prosecutors denied an innocent black man access to evidence which he required to defend himself against wire fraud charge and to present a complete defense which was in violation of his Sixth Amendment rights guaranteed by the U.S. Constitution.

With the fraud case, prior to trial, I made request to Judge Richard J. Sullivan arguing that since I have not been able to obtain the evidence from the Chinese bank, that the Court should exclude testimony from any witness from the bank as it would be patently unfair and highly prejudicial and more importantly would deprive me of my right to a fair trial guaranteed by the United States Constitution. Without the requested evidence the Manhattan prosecutors would be able to freely elicit testimony from the witness while I would be unable to challenge what he said, Judge Sullivan denied my request. Again during trial, when the sole witness from the bank flew from London, U.K. to the U.S to testify about contents of the evidence (the bank's underwriting files which documented the basis for approving the finance), neither the Manhattan prosecutors or the Judge requested the witness to bring along the evidence even though he was about to testify to contents of the evidence (the files). During trial, the witness confirmed that the evidence (the files) existed in London, U.K. and that he was one of the authors of the documents contained in the files. I again made request to the Court to compel for the evidence so that I could use it to defend myself and prove there was no fraud. The Court, who had an obligation to protect my Constitutional rights, instead stated that the witness had testified that the evidence was located in the bank`s file in London, United Kingdom and that the witness had not reviewed the files prior to his testimony and then went on to deny my request for the evidence. This was very wrong because the Court allowed the witness to provide testimony even though the Court itself acknowledged that the witness had not recently reviewed the evidence in preparation for his testimony so he was testifying based on his memory from four years earlier. More importantly, he was allowed to say anything and everything without providing me the opportunity to challenge his testimony or the opportunity for me to be able to present a complete defense.

The Sixth Amendment of the United States Constitution allows all criminal defendants the opportunity to confront witnesses and evidence against them and to have the opportunity to compel for witnesses and evidence in their favor however in this case I have been denied the opportunity to do both when Judge Sullivan denied my several requests for the evidence (the files).

As if that was not bad enough, when the Manhattan prosecutors realized that they were not able to satisfy the law to charge me for bank fraud as it relates to the Chinese bank, they switched to focus on an account which I opened at Morgan Stanley. That account was opened in 2013, with an initial deposit by me, prior to realizing that the bank could not meet my level of service demand and I requested the account closed within a few weeks. The prosecutors argued that I became entitled to "perks" including sky miles, fancy credit card and favorable interest rate by opening the account, however I never used the credit card which I requested closed with zero balance and did not even have the account long enough to earn any sky miles. I never requested for any loan from Morgan Stanley. Most important the Morgan Stanley subsidiary is not federally insured which is required by law to be prosecuted for bank fraud.

When I argued the above, the prosecution then again changed the theory of the bank fraud to a call I made in January 2013 to an employee of another subsidiary of Morgan Stanley to discuss financing about an oil asset. However that employee testified at trial that it was "very preliminary discussion" since it was a single phone call and that he was still confused what the company wanted. He testified that Morgan Stanley would not have been providing any finance since the company was seeking to engage Morgan Stanley to help raise money from third party investors but that it was an initial call to see if both the company and Morgan Stanley wanted to work together. Likewise that Morgan Stanley subsidiary is not federally insured as required by the law.
